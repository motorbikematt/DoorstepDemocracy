During our expansion to process all 88 Ohio counties, we encountered a fascinating data reality: unaffiliated voters aren't necessarily neutral. As we dug into decades of public election history, it became clear that many voters who hadn't participated in recent primaries still exhibited a strong, consistent behavioral partisan history. This exploration led to our first major breakthrough—the creation of the "UNC shadow" metric. By estimating a voter's lifetime Democratic or Republican lean based entirely on their historical ballot behavior, we moved past the limitations of standard registration counts.

This new lens transformed how we view the electorate, providing local organizers with a much sharper picture of their neighborhoods. Instead of treating unaffiliated voters as a monolithic unknown, we can now map out latent support and target outreach more effectively.

**Key Takeaway:** Looking beyond surface-level metrics to understand true behavioral history provides a massive strategic advantage in data-driven organizing.
